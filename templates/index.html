Max Income

    Data Exploration

    I structured this project around the predefined insurance segments. Given established practices in dealing with these various market segments, it would be useful to classify people based on demographic data.

    Since this is a classification problem, our target variable is categorical. Visual inspection depends on whether we're looking at categorical or numeric independent variables (and, for categorical data, how many categories we have.)

    Categorical Data

    I prefer area based visualizations to visualize the effect of categorical variables. With small numbers of categories, I would lean more towards mosaic plots. I chose a stacked bar chart for economic stability because we're looking at a large number of ordinal values. Bars help to define the order, and by representing values allow us to also view the overall distribution of records across economic stability.

    Normalizing the data would make it a bit easier to view the effects, but I think the tradeoff in this case is worth it. Relationships between segments and economic stability are still clear (for example, it's easy to see "Stretched Singles" have higher econoimc stability than "Solid Suburbans")

    This sort of visualization would work for other category-heavy variables, such as youtube rank. For variables with fewer categories such as gender, is_smoker, or is_excerciser, a mosaic plot would be more useful.

    Numeric Data

    Numeric data is better shown by histograms or boxplots. I chose a box plot in this case to easily compare across categories, but could have also used small multiples of histograms for each segment.

    I faced an interesting problem with this variable: income is almost always right skewed. Outliers are normally shown as points outside the whiskers of a boxplot, but the distribution of income would result in a large and potentially distracting number of points above each box. I decided to just exclude outliers in this case, since for visual analysis, the distribution around the interquartile range matters more than the behavior of the outliers (though this would have to be dealt with when building a model.)
